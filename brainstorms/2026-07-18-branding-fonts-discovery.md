## Summary / key decisions
- Target Brand: ZORIXEL (educational brand for web design and AI tools).
- Audience: UI/web designers, freelancers, beginner frontend developers, and vibe coders.

## Q&A log
### Q1 — Brand Personality & Visual Direction
- Asked: Typographic mood/personality for ZORIXEL.
- Captured: The user wants to explore all four options (Modern Futuristic, Elegant Design-First, Bold/Punchy, Quirky/Experimental) across different formats like logos, posters, display cards, and pairings to compare them and make a final branding decision.
- Flags: None.

### Q2 — Copy, Channel Context, and Assets
- Asked: Tagline and copy requirements.
- Captured: The show-reel needs to represent real marketing and content workflows: websites, carousels (IG), posts, and videos (YouTube). The copy should reflect this (branding, design, AI tool content). Background images should be taken from Unsplash.com (or queried via Playwright if needed). The styling should use design tokens, palettes, and rules from the `ui-ux-pro-max`, `design-taste-frontend`, and `impeccable` systems.
- Flags: None.

### Q3 — Presentation Structure & Approach
- Asked: Selected approach from the proposed options.
- Captured: The user chose Approach 1 (The Interactive Route Deck) consisting of a single unified 16:9 presentation showcasing all four routes. The user emphasized that it must not be AI slop and must strictly follow the guidelines of the Premium Frontend Experience System.
- Flags: None.
